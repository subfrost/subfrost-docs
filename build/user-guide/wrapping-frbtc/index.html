Wrapping and Unwrapping frBTC

One of the core functionalities of the SUBFROST ecosystem is the ability to create frBTC, a synthetic asset pegged 1:1 to Bitcoin. This is achieved through a trust-minimized process that locks BTC on the Bitcoin mainnet and mints a corresponding amount of frBTC on the SUBFROST sidechain.

This guide will walk you through the process using the deezel CLI.

The Wrapping Process Explained​

Wrapping involves creating a special Bitcoin transaction that performs two actions simultaneously:

  1. Locks BTC: Your BTC is sent to a secure, multisignature address controlled by the SUBFROST validators.
  2. Mints frBTC: An OP_RETURN output in the same transaction contains instructions for the Alkanes metaprotocol to mint an equivalent amount of frBTC and assign it to your SUBFROST address.

1. Generate a SUBFROST Wallet​

If you haven't already, you need a SUBFROST wallet to receive your frBTC.

deezel wallet create

This command will generate a new wallet and display your address. Keep your mnemonic phrase safe and private.

2. Initiate the Wrap​

To wrap BTC, you will use the deezel wrap command. This command constructs and broadcasts the necessary Bitcoin transaction.

You need to specify three things:

  • The amount of BTC to wrap.
  • Your SUBFROST address (where the frBTC will be sent).
  • The address for receiving transaction change (a regular Bitcoin address).
# Example: Wrap 0.1 BTC
deezel wrap --amount 0.1 --to <your-subfrost-address> --change <your-bitcoin-change-address>

deezel will then guide you through the process, which will involve signing the transaction with a compatible Bitcoin wallet.

3. Confirm Your frBTC Balance​

After the Bitcoin transaction is confirmed, the frBTC will be minted on the SUBFROST chain. You can check your balance using deezel.

deezel balance --address <your-subfrost-address>

You should see your new frBTC balance reflected.

The Unwrapping Process​

Unwrapping is the reverse process: frBTC is burned on the SUBFROST chain, and the equivalent amount of BTC is released from the multisig address back to a Bitcoin address you specify.

1. Initiate the Unwrap​

Use the deezel unwrap command, specifying the amount of frBTC to unwrap and the destination Bitcoin address.

# Example: Unwrap 0.1 frBTC
deezel unwrap --amount 0.1 --to <your-destination-bitcoin-address>

This will create and broadcast a SUBFROST transaction that burns your frBTC.

2. Receive Your BTC​

Once the SUBFROST transaction is finalized, the validators will cooperatively sign a Bitcoin transaction to release the locked BTC to your specified destination address. This process is automated and secured by the FROST protocol.
